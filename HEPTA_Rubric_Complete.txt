QUESTION 1: Object-Centric vs World-Dependent Binding
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
【考查维度】CE (Conceptual Explanation) + TE (Technical Elucidation)

【参考答案】(150 words max):
Object-Centric binding defines gesture location relative to object features (e.g., pinching an object to shrink it), while World-Dependent binding references world/screen features (e.g., dragging to screen edge to delete). Example: Dragging an object—under Object-Centric, the gesture moves the object itself; under World-Dependent, dragging to the top edge might minimize the window. The same physical movement carries different semantic meanings based on the reference frame.

【评分细则】
Score 90-100 (Excellent-CE/TE):
- 准确定义两种binding类型 (Object-Centric: relative to object; World-Dependent: relative to world/screen)
- 使用具体例子清晰展示差异（如拖拽对象本身 vs 拖拽到屏幕边缘）
- 提及"reference frame"或"spatial reference"概念
- 解释为何同一手势在不同binding下功能不同

Score 75-89 (Good-CE/TE):
- 定义基本正确但缺乏技术术语精确性
- 例子恰当但不够具体或仅描述一种binding
- 理解概念但解释reference frame关系不够清晰

Score 60-74 (Satisfactory-CE/TE):
- 混淆两种binding或定义模糊

- 缺少reference frame概念

QUESTION 2: Agreement Score Formula & Widget Implication
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
【考查维度】TE (Technical Elucidation) + TA (Tradeoff Analysis)

【参考答案】(150 words max):
Agreement Score A = Σ(|Pi|/|Pr|)², where Pi is each identical gesture group and Pr is all gestures for a referent. Low agreement indicates users lack shared mental models for that command—they perform different, conflicting gestures. This suggests the command's intended function isn't intuitively matched with a specific physical action. Rather than forcing an arbitrary gesture that users won't guess naturally, designers should use on-screen widgets (buttons/menus) that explicitly show available options, reducing cognitive load and errors.

【评分细则】
Score 90-100 (Excellent-TE/TA):
- 准确写出公式 A = Σ(|Pi|/|Pr|)² 并解释变量含义
- 解释low agreement的含义：用户间缺乏共识/心理模型不一致
- 清晰论证为何需要widget：避免强制任意映射，降低认知负担
- 提及cognitive load或error prevention

Score 75-89 (Good-TE/TA):
- 公式正确但变量解释不够精确
- 理解low agreement含义但解释过于简单
- 能论证widget必要性但缺少认知负荷考量

Score 60-74 (Satisfactory-TE/TA):
- 公式记忆有误但理解大致正确
- 将low agreement误解为其他概念
- widget论证不够有力

Score 0-59 (Poor-TE/TA):

QUESTION 3: Finger Count Flexibility - Designer vs User Defined
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
【考查维度】TA (Tradeoff Analysis) + CE (Conceptual Explanation)

【参考答案】(150 words max):
Designer-defined gestures often strictly differentiate finger counts (1-finger=select, 2-finger=zoom), optimizing for recognition reliability. User-defined gestures show users casually vary 1-3 fingers, treating them as interchangeable "single points." This mismatch occurs because designers prioritize technical discrimination (reliable recognition) while users prioritize cognitive simplicity (natural motor behavior). Systems requiring specific finger counts fail to match user expectations because they violate the "invisibility" of good interaction—users must consciously control finger deployment rather than acting naturally.

【评分细则】
Score 90-100 (Excellent-TA/CE):
- 准确对比设计师（严格区分）vs用户（1-3指随意）的态度差异

- 解释失败原因：违反直觉/增加认知负担/不够"隐形"
- 提及"invisibility"或"natural behavior"

Score 75-89 (Good-TA/CE):

Score 60-74 (Satisfactory-TA/CE):

QUESTION 4: Action Research Spiral Definition
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
【考查维度】CE (Conceptual Explanation) + HCP (Historical Context)

【参考答案】(150 words max):
The Action Research Spiral consists of three core components: Planning (problem formulation and intervention design), Action (implementing the intervention), and Reflection (fact-finding and evaluating effects). Unlike linear hypothesis-testing where researchers test pre-defined hypotheses once, AR iteratively cycles through these stages, with each reflection informing redefined planning. This creates progressive refinement through continuous learning, whereas traditional research seeks to confirm/reject fixed hypotheses without mid-course adaptation. The spiral acknowledges that understanding emerges through doing and that problems evolve as interventions unfold.

【评分细则】
Score 90-100 (Excellent-CE/HCP):

- 清晰对比线性假设检验（一次性验证）vs 螺旋迭代（持续改进）
- 强调"emergence through doing"或"progressive refinement"
- 提及problems evolve/context changes

Score 75-89 (Good-CE/HCP):
- 三阶段正确但对比不够深入
- 理解迭代性但未能强调与传统研究的本质差异

Score 60-74 (Satisfactory-CE/HCP):

Score 0-59 (Poor-CE/HCP):

QUESTION 5: AR vs PD Scope and Goals
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
【考查维度】HCP (Historical Context) + CPI (Cross-Paper Integration)

【参考答案】(150 words max):
Participatory Design (PD) focuses primarily on design processes—engaging users in creating solutions. Action Research (AR) has broader scope: it emphasizes learning through action, constructing scholarly knowledge, and achieving sustainable organizational change. For long-term community projects, AR is superior because PD may end at product delivery, while AR requires researchers to ensure solutions remain viable after departure. AR's "workability" criterion demands practical effectiveness, not just theoretical validity, ensuring technologies are maintained locally rather than creating dependency on external researchers.

QUESTION 6: Friendly Outsider Role & Model Monopoly Prevention
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
【考查维度】CE (Conceptual Explanation) + MA (Methodological Application)

【参考答案】(150 words max):
The "Friendly Outsider" acts as a facilitator/coach rather than expert designer, opening communication lines and validating local knowledge as equally important to scientific expertise. To prevent "Model Monopoly" (researchers dominating through academic credentials), strategies include: explicit power-sharing in decision-making, using participants' language (verbatim principle), member checking (informants verifying data), ensuring alternate contribution methods for non-academic partners, and collaborative writing from project start. The researcher must consciously step back to avoid using argumentation skills and literature knowledge to silence community voices.

QUESTION 7: Capability Uncertainty vs Output Complexity
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
【考查维度】CE (Conceptual Explanation) + TE (Technical Elucidation)

【参考答案】(150 words max):
Capability Uncertainty refers to unpredictability in what AI can do and how well it performs, changing as systems learn from new data. Output Complexity concerns the variability of possible outputs, ranging from few fixed options to infinite adaptive possibilities. Capability Uncertainty complicates prototyping because designers cannot anticipate performance degradation or improvement over time—what works today may fail tomorrow as the system evolves. Output Complexity complicates sketching because designers cannot enumerate all possible system behaviors to create comprehensive wireframes, especially for Level 4 systems generating context-dependent infinite variations.

【评分细则】

- 分别解释对原型设计的影响（Capability→时间维度/演化；Output→空间维度/枚举）
- 提及Level 4系统或context-dependent variation
- 区分clearly: 时间vs空间维度

Score 75-89 (Good-CE/TE):

Score 60-74 (Satisfactory-CE/TE):

QUESTION 8: Sketching Difficulty for Level 4 Systems
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
【考查维度】TE (Technical Elucidation) + TA (Tradeoff Analysis)

【参考答案】(150 words max):
Level 4 systems (Evolving Adaptive) combine continuous learning with infinite adaptive outputs. Traditional sketching fails because: (1) Static representations cannot capture temporal evolution—system capabilities change post-deployment as they learn; (2) Finite sketches cannot represent infinite output possibilities that vary by user/context; (3) Two-way adaptation (user-system co-evolution) creates emergent behaviors impossible to predict from initial designs. Unlike static UIs where designers can wireframe all states, Level 4 systems are "living" sociotechnical systems requiring prototyping methods that accommodate uncertainty and evolution, such as longitudinal field trials rather than fixed prototypes.

【评分细则】
Score 90-100 (Excellent-TE/TA):
- 准确识别Level 4特征（evolving + adaptive/infinite）
- 列出3个失败原因：静态无法捕捉时间演化、有限无法表示无限、双向适应产生涌现行为
- 提及"living system"或"sociotechnical"概念
- 提出替代方案（longitudinal trials）

Score 75-89 (Good-TE/TA):
- 识别特征正确但原因分析不够全面
- 理解静态限制但缺少涌现行为分析

Score 60-74 (Satisfactory-TE/TA):
- 仅描述Level 4但未解释为何草图失效

QUESTION 9: Synthesis - AR for User-Defined Gestures in AI Systems
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
【考查维度】CPI (Cross-Paper Integration) + MA (Methodological Application)

【参考答案】(150 words max):
Action Research methodology could improve gesture set design for AI-powered surfaces by addressing Yang's "Capability Uncertainty" through the AR spiral. Rather than one-time gesture elicitation (Wobbrock), the AR spiral enables longitudinal cycles: Plan (design initial gesture set), Act (deploy with users), Reflect (analyze how gestures evolve as AI adapts). This captures user-system co-evolution—how user gestures change as the AI's recognition capabilities evolve, and vice versa. For Level 4 systems, AR's "Prolonged Engagement" ensures researchers observe emergent interaction patterns over time, while "Member Checking" verifies that gesture interpretations remain valid as the system learns, addressing the situated capability uncertainty that static user studies miss.

【评分细则】
Score 90-100 (Excellent-CPI/MA):
- 明确连接AR螺旋与Yang的Capability Uncertainty
- 具体展示如何将AR应用于手势研究（Plan-Act-Reflect替代一次性elicitation）
- 解释user-system co-evolution在手势语境中的含义
- 引用Prolonged Engagement或Member Checking解决Level 4挑战
- 三篇论文整合流畅（Wobbrock方法+Hayes方法论+Yang问题框架）

Score 75-89 (Good-CPI/MA):
- 能连接AR与手势研究但缺少具体机制
